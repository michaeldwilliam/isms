1. Scope & relationship to the Business Impact Analysis (BIA)
This plan covers payment processing and transaction routing, banking integrations and settlement, and identity and access management for Monarch Consolidated Ltd, an FCA-regulated payments business. Recovery targets from the Business Impact Analysis: payment processing has a 1 hour recovery target and a 5 minute data-loss target; banking integrations a 2 hour target and 15 minute data-loss target. Financial Conduct Authority (United Kingdom) (FCA) operational-resilience expectations apply.
2. Invocation & escalation
The Business Continuity Plan is invoked when a disruption prevents normal operation of a critical process beyond its tolerable downtime, for example a major system outage, ransomware, loss of premises, or loss of a key supplier. The incident lead declares invocation, convenes the recovery team, and owns the recovery decision. Stand-down is declared once critical processes are restored and verified.
3. Communications during disruption
During an incident the communications lead keeps staff, affected customers and, where required, regulators informed using a pre-agreed channel and holding statements. Contact details are held in the table below and kept current. Notifications required by law or contract are made within their deadlines.
