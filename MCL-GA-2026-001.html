Executive summary
Monarch Consolidated runs a regulated UK payments platform, and its ISMS is already well past the typical first-applicant baseline: cryptography, access control, and logging are sound, and the internal audit and management review cycles are running. What stands between the organisation and a Stage 2 pass is a short list of three P1 items: the shared DBA credential on the legacy production database, the absence of enforced MFA on the payment dashboard and ops portal, and — under Cl. 6 — the absence of both a Statement of Applicability and formal treatment for the March 2026 penetration-test findings, which are recorded in the risk register (R03) but lack formal ISMS treatment decisions and acceptance records. None of the three is structural; each can be closed inside a 30-day window. On that basis a Q4 2026 Stage 2 audit is achievable.
