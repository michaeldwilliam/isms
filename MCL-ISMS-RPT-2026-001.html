ISMS Engagement Report
Monarch Consolidated Ltd
MCL-ISMS-RPT-2026-001 · 2026-06-06 · CONFIDENTIAL — CLIENT AND ASSESSOR ONLY
1. Executive Summary
Monarch Consolidated Ltd is an FCA-regulated payments provider operating a card-not-present (online) payment processing platform for UK and EU business clients. Monarch Consolidated engaged an independent ISO/IEC 27001 implementer to assess its ISMS against ISO/IEC 27001:2022 ahead of a planned Stage 2 certification audit, and to confirm alignment with overlapping PCI DSS v4.0.1 and FCA operational-resilience obligations. The assessment found a mature security posture: documented policies, role-based access control, strong cryptography, centralised logging, an operating internal-audit and management-review cadence, and a measured IS objectives programme. Monarch Consolidated sits well ahead of where a first-time applicant usually starts. Three findings nonetheless represent certification blockers and are rated P1: (1) a shared database administrator credential on the legacy production instance, eliminating individual accountability; (2) multi-factor authentication not enforced on the customer payment dashboard or internal operations portal; and (3) the ISMS had no Statement of Applicability at assessment — a draft (MCL-SOA-2026-001) has since been produced under this engagement and awaits management adoption — and the March 2026 penetration-test findings, while logged in the risk register (R03), lacked formal treatment decisions at assessment — these have since been drafted under this engagement (MCL-RR-2026-001 / MCL-RTP-2026-001) and await risk-owner approval and acceptance records (Cl. 6.1.3(f)). Each is independently remediable within 30 days. With the P1 items closed and the supporting P2 actions (board mandate for the CISO, supplier IS schedules, DLP egress coverage, FCA operational-resilience mapping, and SIEM coverage extension) addressed, Monarch Consolidated is assessed as capable of passing a Stage 2 audit in Q4 2026 with Bureau Veritas.
2. Scope and Methodology
Scope: Monarch Consolidated's payment processing API, the cardholder data environment (CDE), AWS infrastructure, the CI/CD pipeline, the Modulr and Stripe Connect banking integrations, the Datadog SIEM, and the internal operations portal. London head office and UK-based remote staff (85 personnel). Data types: cardholder data, customer financial records, and UK/EU personal data subject to UK GDPR. Exclusions: client-side integrations and the physical premises of banking partners. Methodology: document review, structured interviews with the CISO, CTO, and DPO, control-evidence sampling, and review of the March 2026 penetration-test report and the second internal-audit cycle. All seven mandatory clauses (Cl. 4–10) were assessed, with 22 of 93 Annex A controls sampled on a risk basis (FCA regulatory obligations, PCI DSS v4.0.1 overlap, and highest residual risk). Physical controls (A.7) were not sampled — lower residual risk in the fully cloud-hosted model — with A.7.6, A.7.11 and A.7.12 justified not applicable in the Statement of Applicability. Engagement period: 2026-05-18 to 2026-06-06.
3. Key Findings and Risk Posture
Maturity: of the mandatory clauses, five of seven are fully conformant; Cl. 5 (Leadership) is partial — the CISO board mandate and IS policy approval are not formally documented; Cl. 6 (Planning) is non-conformant — no Statement of Applicability existed at assessment (a draft has since been produced and awaits adoption) and risk treatment formalisation is incomplete. Across the assessed Annex A controls, the majority are conformant or partial, with two non-conformities (A.8.2 and A.8.5). A third certification-blocking nonconformity sits at clause level (Cl. 6 Planning), giving three P1 findings in total. P1 (certification blockers): - A.8.2 Privileged access: shared DBA credential on the legacy production database. - A.8.5 Secure authentication: MFA not enforced on the payment dashboard or ops portal (pen test high finding). - Cl. 6.1.3 / Cl. 6: no Statement of Applicability existed at assessment (draft MCL-SOA-2026-001 now produced, pending adoption); pen-test findings are logged in the risk register (R03); treatment decisions have been drafted (MCL-RTP-2026-001) and await risk-owner approval and acceptance records. A.8.8 (two high-severity pen-test findings open past the 30-day SLA) is rated P2 but its remediation is accelerated into Phase 1, as the SLA is already breached. P2 (required before Stage 2): - Cl. 5 / A.5.1: CISO board mandate and IS policy approval not formally documented. - A.5.19: Modulr and Stripe Connect MSAs lack a formal IS schedule. - A.8.12: DLP coverage of data-egress paths incomplete. - A.5.30: FCA operational-resilience impact-tolerance mapping and tested DR runbook outstanding. - A.8.16: SIEM detection coverage at ~60% of OWASP Top 10; extension to 90%+ with a documented use-case catalogue in progress.
4. Remediation Roadmap
Phase 1 (Days 1–30): Replace the shared DBA credential with individual accounts; enforce MFA on the dashboard and ops portal; secure management adoption and approval of the drafted Statement of Applicability (MCL-SOA-2026-001); obtain risk-owner approval and acceptance records for the drafted pen-test treatment decisions (R03); remediate and re-test the two open high findings. Phase 2 (Days 31–60): Obtain the board resolution for the CISO mandate and IS policy approval; append IS schedules to banking-partner MSAs; extend DLP egress coverage; complete FCA op-res impact-tolerance mapping and a tested DR runbook; extend SIEM coverage to 90%+. Phase 3 (Days 61–90): Enforce secure code-review gates across all repositories; standardise the SDLC security gate; complete the third internal audit cycle. Phase 4 (Days 91–180): Independent ISMS review; engage Bureau Veritas for the Stage 2 audit.
5. Certification Readiness Assessment
Readiness is high: of the 29 requirements assessed, 16 are fully conformant and 10 are partial, with the three non-conformities corresponding to the P1 findings. The ISMS framework, documentation, and operating cadence are in place. Readiness is gated on closing the three P1 findings (shared DBA credential, dashboard MFA, and adoption of the drafted SoA with risk-register integration) and the five P2 actions. On the remediation timeline provided, a Q4 2026 Stage 2 audit with Bureau Veritas is achievable.
6. Recommendations
1. Close the three P1 findings within 30 days; each is an independent certification blocker. 2. Adopt and approve the drafted Statement of Applicability (MCL-SOA-2026-001) covering all 93 controls; it is both a mandatory document and the spine of the audit evidence. 3. Obtain risk-owner approval and formal acceptance records for the drafted treatment decisions covering the March 2026 penetration-test findings (R03); close the two open high findings within 30 days. 4. Formalise governance: a documented board resolution for the CISO mandate and IS policy approval. 5. Complete FCA operational-resilience impact-tolerance mapping to satisfy the overlapping regulatory obligation. 6. Engage the certification body early to confirm scope and schedule the Stage 2 audit.
