ISO/IEC 27001:2022 ISMS Engagement Report
Monarch Consolidated Ltd
MCL-ISMS-RPT-2026-001 · 2026-06-06
1. Executive Summary
Monarch Consolidated Ltd is an FCA-regulated payments provider operating a card-not-present (online) payment processing platform for UK and EU business clients. Monarch Consolidated engaged an independent ISO/IEC 27001 implementer to assess its ISMS against ISO/IEC 27001:2022 ahead of a planned Stage 2 certification audit, and to confirm alignment with overlapping PCI DSS v4.0 and FCA operational-resilience obligations. The assessment found a mature security posture: documented policies, role-based access control, strong cryptography, centralised logging, an operating internal-audit and management-review cadence, and a measured IS objectives programme. Monarch Consolidated sits well ahead of where a first-time applicant usually starts. Three findings nonetheless represent certification blockers and are rated P1: (1) a shared database administrator credential on the legacy production instance, eliminating individual accountability; (2) multi-factor authentication not enforced on the customer payment dashboard or internal operations portal; and (3) no Statement of Applicability has been produced, and while the March 2026 penetration-test findings are logged in the risk register (R03), they have not been assigned formal treatment decisions or an acceptance record within the ISMS. Each is independently remediable within 30 days. With the P1 items closed and the supporting P2 actions (board mandate for the CISO, supplier IS schedules, DLP egress coverage, and FCA operational-resilience mapping) addressed, Monarch Consolidated is assessed as capable of passing a Stage 2 audit in Q4 2026 with Bureau Veritas.
2. Scope and Methodology
Systems and processes: Payment processing API, cardholder data environment (CDE), AWS infrastructure, CI/CD pipeline, Modulr and Stripe Connect integrations, Datadog SIEM, internal ops portal. Locations: London head office; remote staff across UK. People and roles: All 85 staff including engineering, operations, compliance, and customer success. Data types: Cardholder data, customer financial records, UK and EU personal data subject to UK GDPR
3. Key Findings and Risk Posture
Maturity: of the mandatory clauses, five of seven are fully conformant; Cl. 5 (Leadership) is partial — the CISO board mandate and IS policy approval are not formally documented; Cl. 6 (Planning) is non-conformant — no Statement of Applicability has been produced and risk treatment formalisation is incomplete. Across the assessed Annex A controls, the majority are conformant or partial, with two non-conformities (A.8.2 and A.8.5). A third certification-blocking nonconformity sits at clause level (Cl. 6 Planning), giving three P1 findings in total. P1 (certification blockers): - A.8.2 Privileged access: shared DBA credential on the legacy production database. - A.8.5 Secure authentication: MFA not enforced on the payment dashboard or ops portal (pen test high finding). - Cl. 6.1.3 / Cl. 6: no Statement of Applicability produced; pen-test findings are logged in the risk register (R03) but formal treatment decisions and acceptance records are absent from the ISMS. P2 (required before Stage 2): - Cl. 5 / A.5.1: CISO board mandate and IS policy approval not formally documented. - A.5.19: Modulr and Stripe Connect MSAs lack a formal IS schedule. - A.8.12: DLP coverage of data-egress paths incomplete. - A.5.30: FCA operational-resilience impact-tolerance mapping and tested DR runbook outstanding. - A.8.8: two high-severity pen test findings open past the 30-day SLA.
4. Remediation Roadmap
Phase 1 (0-30 days): Replace the shared DBA credential with individual accounts; enforce MFA on the dashboard and ops portal; produce the Statement of Applicability; formalise treatment decisions and acceptance records for the registered pen-test risks (R03); remediate and re-test the two open high findings. Phase 2 (30-60 days): Obtain the board resolution for the CISO mandate and IS policy approval; append IS schedules to banking-partner MSAs; extend DLP egress coverage; complete FCA op-res impact-tolerance mapping and a tested DR runbook; extend SIEM coverage to 90%+. Phase 3 (60-90 days): Enforce secure code-review gates across all repositories; standardise the SDLC security gate; complete the third internal audit cycle. Phase 4 (90-120 days): Independent ISMS review; engage Bureau Veritas for the Stage 2 audit.
5. Certification Readiness Assessment
Readiness is high, with roughly 80% of Stage 2 requirements already satisfied. The ISMS framework, documentation, and operating cadence are in place. Readiness is gated on closing the three P1 findings (shared DBA credential, dashboard MFA, and the SoA / risk-register integration) and the five P2 actions. On the remediation timeline provided, a Q4 2026 Stage 2 audit with Bureau Veritas is achievable.
6. Recommendations
1. Close the three P1 findings within 30 days; each is an independent certification blocker. 2. Produce the Statement of Applicability covering all 93 controls; it is both a mandatory document and the spine of the audit evidence. 3. Formalise treatment decisions and acceptance records for the March 2026 penetration-test findings already registered as R03; close the two open high findings within 30 days. 4. Formalise governance: a documented board resolution for the CISO mandate and IS policy approval. 5. Complete FCA operational-resilience impact-tolerance mapping to satisfy the overlapping regulatory obligation. 6. Engage the certification body early to confirm scope and schedule the Stage 2 audit.
