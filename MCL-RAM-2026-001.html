Risk Assessment Methodology
Monarch Consolidated Ltd · ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Clause 6.1.2
MCL-RAM-2026-001 · v1.0 · 2026-06-20
1. Purpose and scope
This methodology defines how information security risks within the Information Security Management System (ISMS) scope are identified, analysed, evaluated and treated for Monarch Consolidated Ltd. It applies to all information assets, processes, systems and locations covered by the Information Security Management System (ISMS) scope statement. It exists so that risk decisions are made on a consistent, repeatable basis that produces comparable results over time.
2. Risk identification approach
Risks are identified using an asset-based approach. For each information asset in the Asset Inventory, applicable threats and vulnerabilities are considered, drawing on incident history, the supplier register, vulnerability findings, and input from the people who run each process. New risks are also captured from entries in the Change Management Log and from internal audit findings.
3. Risk analysis approach
Each risk is assessed for likelihood and impact using the 1 to 5 scales below. The risk level is the product of the likelihood and impact ratings, read from the 5 by 5 matrix, giving a value between 1 and 25. The same scales are used for inherent risk before controls and residual risk after controls, so the effect of treatment is measurable.
4. Risk evaluation and prioritisation
Risks are prioritised by risk level. Each level maps to a band: Low 1 to 4, Medium 5 to 9, High 10 to 15, Critical 16 to 25. High and Critical risks require treatment. Low and Medium risks may be accepted in line with the acceptance criteria below. The prioritised list drives the Risk Treatment Plan.
5. Risk acceptance criteria
Residual risks rated Low or Medium may be accepted by the Chief Information Security Officer (CISO). Residual risks rated High require acceptance by the CEO with documented justification recorded in the Risk Acceptance Record. Critical residual risks may not be accepted and must be treated further until they fall to High or below. The risk acceptance threshold for this Information Security Management System (ISMS) is a residual score of 9.
6. Roles and responsibilities
The Chief Information Security Officer (CISO) owns the risk process and maintains the Risk Register. Risk owners named in the register are accountable for treating their risks. The CEO and Board approve High residual risks and review the risk profile at each management review.
7. Defined scales and risk matrix
Likelihood scale (1 to 5)
1 — RareRare: not expected within the Information Security Management System (ISMS) review cycle.
2 — UnlikelyUnlikely: could occur but not expected.
3 — PossiblePossible: may occur within the review cycle.
4 — LikelyLikely: expected to occur within the review cycle.
5 — Almost certainAlmost certain: expected to occur repeatedly.
Impact scale (1 to 5)
1 — NegligibleNegligible: no meaningful effect on operations, data or reputation.
2 — MinorMinor: limited, short-term disruption recovered with routine effort.
3 — ModerateModerate: noticeable disruption, some data or service loss, manageable cost.
4 — MajorMajor: significant breach or outage, regulatory exposure, real financial or reputational harm.
5 — SevereSevere: critical breach or sustained outage threatening the viability of the business.
