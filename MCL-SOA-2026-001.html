Cl.6.1.3 note: This Statement of Applicability records the organisation's decisions on which ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Annex A controls are applicable to the ISMS scope. Controls marked Not Applicable must have a documented justification. All exclusions are subject to review by the certification body during Stage 1 audit. Controls marked Applicable must be addressed through the risk treatment plan. This document must be maintained and updated as the ISMS matures.

Theme 5 — Organisational controls
A.5.1Policies for information securityYesIS policy suite (6 documents) exists and is mandated under FCA SYSC and ISO 27001 Cl.5.2. Board has not formally approved the latest revision — approval scheduled.In progressCISOPolicy register v3.2; board agenda Q3

Theme 7 — Physical controls
A.7.1Physical security perimetersYesThe London office is a serviced facility; building perimeter and reception controls are operated by the landlord and relied on as supplier-provided controls under the membership agreement. No production systems are hosted on site.ImplementedCISOServiced-office membership agreement
A.7.2Physical entryYesOffice entry is keycard-controlled with visitor sign-in, managed by the landlord; the access list for the company suite is reviewed periodically.ImplementedCISOLandlord access records; suite access list
A.7.3Securing offices, rooms and facilitiesYesThe company occupies a dedicated lockable suite within the serviced facility; no cardholder data is processed or stored on site.ImplementedCISOOffice layout; suite licence terms
A.7.4Physical security monitoringYesLandlord-operated CCTV and access logging cover the building; an escalation route for physical-security incidents is defined in the IR plan.ImplementedCISOLandlord security statement; IR plan v3
A.7.5Protecting against physical and environmental threatsYesFire, flood, and environmental protections are provided at building level by the landlord; with no on-premise infrastructure, residual exposure is limited to endpoint loss, mitigated by full-disk encryption and backups.ImplementedCISOBuilding facilities statement; MDM FDE report
A.7.6Working in secure areasNoNo designated secure areas exist: production infrastructure is cloud-hosted (AWS) and the office is a serviced facility where no cardholder data is processed or stored. Data-centre secure-area controls fall under the AWS shared-responsibility model (SOC 2 / ISO 27001 certified).Not applicable—AWS shared responsibility model; serviced-office agreement
A.7.7Clear desk and clear screenYesClear desk and clear screen policy applies to the office and to remote workers; automatic screen-lock is enforced via MDM.ImplementedCISOIS policy suite; MDM screen-lock config

A.7.9Security of assets off-premisesYesRemote staff laptops are off-premises assets by default: full-disk encryption, MDM enrolment, and remote-wipe capability are mandatory; loss and theft reporting is defined in the IR plan.ImplementedCISOMDM inventory; FDE compliance report

A.7.11Supporting utilitiesNoNo owned information-processing facility: supporting utilities for production systems are AWS responsibility under the shared-responsibility model; office utilities are provided and maintained by the serviced-office landlord.Not applicable—AWS shared responsibility model; serviced-office agreement
A.7.12Cabling securityNoNo owned premises cabling: data-centre cabling is AWS responsibility under the shared-responsibility model; office network cabling is provided and managed by the serviced-office landlord.Not applicable—AWS shared responsibility model; serviced-office agreement

Theme 8 — Technological controls
A.8.1User endpoint devicesYesThe endpoint estate is fully managed via MDM: full-disk encryption, screen-lock, patch enforcement, and EDR on all laptops; BYOD is prohibited.ImplementedCISOMDM compliance dashboard
A.8.2Privileged access rightsYesPrivileged access management is applicable and critical. Two DBAs share a single admin account on the legacy production PostgreSQL instance — no individual accountability. Migration to individual least-privilege accounts is in progress (R01) and remains a certification blocker until closed.In progressCISOPen test Mar 2026 finding #1; IAM review
A.8.3Information access restrictionYesAccess to information is restricted per classification and role; cardholder-data access is limited to named operations staff and logged.ImplementedCISOIAM policy; CHD access list
A.8.4Access to source codeYesSource access is via the GitHub organisation with SSO and MFA; branch protection and CODEOWNERS are enforced; tokens are scoped and rotated.ImplementedCTOGitHub organisation settings
A.8.5Secure authenticationYesMFA enforced on AWS, GitHub, and Google Workspace. NOT enforced on the customer payment dashboard or internal ops portal — pen test high finding. Conditional Access rollout in progress.In progressCISOPen test Mar 2026 finding #2; Microsoft Entra CA policy
A.8.6Capacity managementYesCapacity is monitored in Datadog with AWS auto-scaling on the payment platform; capacity is reviewed at the monthly ops meeting.ImplementedCTODatadog capacity dashboards
A.8.7Protection against malwareYesEDR is deployed fleet-wide with central alerting, layered with email filtering and user awareness training.ImplementedCISOEDR console; email filter config
A.8.8Management of technical vulnerabilitiesYesSnyk SAST in CI/CD; monthly vulnerability scans. Two high-severity pen test findings remain open past the 30-day SLA — being remediated. Patch SLA defined.In progressCISOSnyk dashboard; pen test remediation tracker
A.8.9Configuration managementYesInfrastructure is defined as code (Terraform) with peer review; AWS Config rules and Wiz detect drift from the documented hardening baseline.ImplementedCTOTerraform repository; AWS Config rules
A.8.10Information deletionYesDeletion is performed per the retention schedule; a customer-data deletion workflow is implemented, with cryptographic erasure via KMS key retirement.ImplementedCISORetention schedule; deletion SOP
A.8.11Data maskingYesCardholder data is tokenised at capture; PAN is masked in user interfaces and logs per PCI DSS; production data is not used for testing (see A.8.33).ImplementedCISOTokenisation design doc; PCI DSS ROC
A.8.12Data leakage preventionYesCardholder data flows mapped for PCI DSS. DLP tooling partially deployed (email + endpoint); coverage of data egress paths being extended.In progressCISOPCI DSS ROC; DLP rollout plan
A.8.13Information backupYesAutomated daily backups are encrypted and replicated multi-AZ; quarterly restore tests are evidenced.ImplementedCISOBackup policy; Q1 2026 restore test
A.8.14Redundancy of information processing facilitiesYesMulti-AZ deployment with automated failover for the payment platform; redundancy requirements are defined per service tier.ImplementedCTOArchitecture overview; AWS configuration
A.8.15LoggingYesCentralised logging via Datadog. Log retention 13 months; review SOP defined; alerting configured.ImplementedCISODatadog config; log review SOP
A.8.16Monitoring activitiesYesDatadog SIEM live with ~60% OWASP Top 10 alert coverage; engineering on-call triages. Extension to 90%+ coverage and a documented use-case catalogue in progress.In progressCISOSIEM use-case catalogue
A.8.17Clock synchronizationYesTime is synchronised via AWS Time Sync across all instances; log timestamps are normalised to UTC in Datadog.ImplementedCISOInstance baseline configuration
A.8.18Use of privileged utility programsYesPrivileged utility and administrative tooling is restricted to break-glass roles; usage is logged and alerted in Datadog.ImplementedCISOIAM policy; Datadog alert rules
A.8.19Installation of software on operational systemsYesProduction changes are deployed only through the CI/CD pipeline from approved base images; direct software installation on production is blocked.ImplementedCTOPipeline configuration
A.8.20Networks securityYesVPC segmentation with deny-by-default security groups and WAF on public endpoints; the CDE network is segmented in line with PCI DSS.ImplementedCISONetwork architecture; segmentation test
A.8.21Security of network servicesYesSecurity requirements for network services are defined; provider services (AWS, CDN) are covered by agreements and monitored.ImplementedCISONetwork services catalogue
A.8.22Segregation of networksYesThe CDE is segregated from corporate and development networks; segmentation is validated by the annual PCI penetration test.ImplementedCISOSegmentation test report
A.8.23Web filteringYesDNS and web filtering are enforced on managed endpoints with categories blocked per the acceptable use policy.ImplementedCISOFiltering configuration
A.8.24Use of cryptographyYesTLS 1.3 in transit; AES-256 at rest on all RDS/S3. Documented cryptographic standard v2.1; KMS key rotation enforced.ImplementedCISOCrypto standard v2.1; KMS config
A.8.25Secure development life cycleYesSecure SDLC policy exists; threat modelling on major features. Mandatory secure code review gates not yet enforced across all repositories in CI/CD.In progressCTOSDLC policy; CI/CD pipeline config
A.8.26Application security requirementsYesSecurity requirements are defined per feature in the SDLC, with PCI DSS and UK GDPR requirements traced to implementation.ImplementedCTOSDLC policy; requirements template
A.8.27Secure system architecture and engineering principlesYesSecurity architecture principles are documented (least privilege, defence in depth, zero-trust access) and applied in design reviews.ImplementedCTOArchitecture standards
A.8.28Secure codingYesSecure coding standards documented (OWASP ASVS-aligned). Enforcement via mandatory review is inconsistent across teams — being standardised.In progressCTOSecure coding standard; PR review metrics
A.8.29Security testing in development and acceptanceYesSAST (Snyk) runs in CI, DAST pre-release, and an annual external penetration test; security checks are included in acceptance criteria.ImplementedCTOCI configuration; pen test Mar 2026
A.8.30Outsourced developmentYesAll development is in-house; contingent IS requirements for any future outsourced development are defined in the procurement standard.ImplementedCTOProcurement standard
A.8.31Separation of development, test and production environmentsYesDev, test, and production fully separated across AWS accounts with no data flow from prod to lower environments.ImplementedCISOAWS account structure; network diagram
A.8.32Change managementYesChanges are managed through the CI/CD pipeline with approval gates; an emergency-change procedure with retrospective review is defined.ImplementedCTOChange records
A.8.33Test informationYesProduction cardholder data and PII are prohibited in lower environments; synthetic test data is generated, enforced by environment separation (A.8.31).ImplementedCTOTest-data standard
A.8.34Protection of information systems during audit testingYesAudit and test access is time-boxed, read-only where possible, and CISO-approved; a penetration-test rules-of-engagement template is in use.ImplementedCISOPen test RoE Mar 2026
