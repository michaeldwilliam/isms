Proof a company takes data security seriously.

ISO 27001 is the certificate that shows a company manages its data security properly — checked by an independent auditor. Banks, big clients, and regulators often won't sign a deal until they see it, or at least see that you're on track to get it. This portfolio walks through the core documentation a project produces, using a made-up UK fintech company as the example.

MCL-GA-2026-001

Gap Analysis Report

Think of this as a health check. It looks at how the company protects its data today and lines that up against what the certificate actually requires. The client finds out exactly where they fall short (here, 29 areas checked and three serious gaps found) and gets a clear to-do list, sorted by what matters most. The point is simple: fix the problems quietly now, instead of getting caught out by an inspector later.

Open document
MCL-RR-2026-001 2026-05-25

Risk Register

A list of everything that could go wrong with the company's data, and how bad each one would be. Things like a hacker getting in through a supplier, old software no one updated, or the wrong people having access. Each danger gets a score, a person whose job it is to handle it, and a plan. It turns a vague worry ("could we get hacked?") into something the company can actually act on.

Open document
MCL-SOA-2026-001 2026-05-29

Statement of Applicability

There's an official checklist of 93 security measures a company can use, from locking server rooms to training staff. This document goes through every single one and says whether the company uses it, and if not, why not. When the inspector shows up, this is one of the first things they ask for. Having it ready and honest saves days of awkward questions.

Open document
MCL-RTP-2026-001 2026-06-01

Risk Treatment Plan

Knowing the risks is one thing. Actually fixing them is another. This takes each danger from the risk list and turns it into a real job: what needs doing, who's doing it, by when, and how you'll know it's done. It's the difference between a nice report that sits in a drawer and one that actually gets the company safer.

Open document
MCL-ISMS-RPT-2026-001 2026-06-06

Engagement Report

The summary that goes to the people running the company, the ones who pay for all this. No tech speak. It says plainly: here's what we looked at, here's what we found, here's the risk you're carrying, and here's whether you're ready to pass. Written so a busy boss can read it in five minutes and know exactly where things stand.

What this is

Most companies don't chase ISO 27001 for fun. Usually a big client demanded it, a regulator flagged a problem, or something went wrong and the question could no longer be ignored. The work itself is manageable once you know the standard, but there's a lot of it. Monarch Consolidated Ltd is a made-up UK fintech I built to show what the core documentation looks like across all five deliverables.

The five documents build on each other. The health check sets the baseline. The risk list ranks the dangers. The applicability statement records which security measures are in use. The action plan assigns the fixes. The final report ties it all together for the people in charge. Each one is built to stand on its own and survive a close look from an outside inspector.
